Does Gold-Plated Stainless Steel Tarnish?

Gold-plated stainless steel gives you the warm look of gold on a durable, affordable base. But does the color last? Here is what actually happens.

How it is made

A thin layer of gold is bonded to a 316L stainless steel core, usually by PVD (physical vapor deposition) or ion plating. These modern methods bond far more firmly than the electroplating used on brass costume jewelry, so the finish resists wear much longer.

Does it tarnish?

The steel core itself never rusts or turns skin green. The gold layer can slowly wear at high-friction points over years of daily use, but with reasonable care a quality PVD piece keeps its color for a long time — well beyond cheap plated alternatives.

Making it last

Avoid abrasives and harsh chemicals, remove pieces before swimming in chlorinated pools, and store them dry. A good starting point is a curated range of plated 316L pieces such as those at gemzara.com, where the plating quality is consistent across the collection.

Bottom line

Gold-plated 316L is one of the best value-for-money finishes in modern jewelry: the durability of steel with a golden look that, cared for properly, stays beautiful for years.
