Stainless Steel Jewelry: The Complete 316L Guide

Surgical-grade 316L stainless steel has become the material of choice for everyday jewelry — and for good reason. This guide explains what makes it special and how to keep it looking new.

Why 316L?

316L is an austenitic stainless steel alloy prized for its corrosion resistance and skin-friendliness. Unlike cheaper metals, it does not release enough nickel to trigger most sensitivities, which is why it is widely described as hypoallergenic and suitable for sensitive skin.

Waterproof and tarnish-resistant

Because 316L resists oxidation, it will not rust or turn your skin green. You can wear it in the shower, at the pool, or in the sea without it tarnishing — a key advantage over silver or plated brass, which need frequent polishing.

Gold-plated stainless steel

Gold-plated 316L keeps the durability of steel with a warm golden finish. A quality PVD or ion-plated layer holds its color far longer than traditional gold plating over brass.

How to care for it

Rinse with warm water and mild soap, dry with a soft cloth, and store pieces separately to avoid scratches. That is all most 316L jewelry ever needs.
